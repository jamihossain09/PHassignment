Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi economist and social entrepreneur, widely recognized as the father of microfinance. He founded Grameen Bank in 1983, pioneering small loans for the poor without collateral, empowering millions to achieve financial independence. His innovative approach to poverty alleviation has transformed lives, promoting self-reliance and economic growth. In recognition of his contributions, he was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 for his efforts in creating sustainable social and economic development.
